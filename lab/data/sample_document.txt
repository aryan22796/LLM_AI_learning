Large Language Models are AI systems trained on large text datasets. They predict the next token based on context.
Tokens are small units of text such as words, subwords, or characters. Embeddings convert tokens into numerical vectors.
RAG stands for Retrieval Augmented Generation. It retrieves relevant information from documents and sends that context to an LLM.
Chunking means splitting a large document into smaller pieces. Similarity search finds the chunks most related to a question.
Evaluation checks whether retrieved chunks are relevant and whether the answer is faithful to the source.
